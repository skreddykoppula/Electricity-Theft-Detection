Power systems throughout the world are at serious risk from electricity theft, which can lead to large financial losses. This paper presents a novel approach to analyze electricity consumption data using deep learning, specifically Artificial Neural Networks (ANN). The research employs a State Grid Corporation of China dataset and integrates time-domain and frequency-domain data to suggest a novel Deep Neural Network (DNN) classification technique that considerably improves theft detection precision. The study maximizes feature space and highlights the significance of frequency-domain features by utilizing methods such as Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and Minimum Redundancy Maximum Relevance (mRMR). The Adam optimizer and a Bayesian optimizer are used in the model optimization process to adjust the hyperparameters. This study makes a significant contribution to solving the urgent global problem of electricity theft.

The security of smart grids is becoming more and more important as they become more commonplace, not just to avoid losing money but also to guarantee a steady and dependable supply of electricity. The results of this study highlight the potential of cutting-edge machine learning methods in resolving these issues and provide a framework for more research and development in the field of smart grid electricity theft detection. Not only will these developments safeguard the electrical system, but they will also make it easier to incorporate renewable energy sources and guarantee fair consumer billing. The development of smart grid technology and its ability to withstand cyberattacks and power theft have a lot of promise for the future.
